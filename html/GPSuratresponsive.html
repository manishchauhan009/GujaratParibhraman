Introduction

Surat is a city in the western Indian state of Gujarat. The word Surat literally means face in Gujarati and Hindi. Located on the banks of the river Tapti near its confluence with the Arabian Sea, it used to be a large seaport. It is now the commercial and economic center in South Gujarat, and one of the largest urban areas of western India. It has well-established diamond and textile industry, and is a major supply centre for apparels and accessories. About 90% of the world's diamonds supply are cut and polished in the city.

Use Road

Surat lies 234 km from Ahmedabad, 154 km from Vadodara, and 279 km from Mumbai. Bus stations, both ST and private, are on the eastern edge of the city.

Use Train

Surat in on the main broad gauge line between Mumbai and Ahmedabad.

Use Airplane

Various domestic flights connecting metros and other major cities are operational from the Surat Airport.

GOPI TALAV

Gopi Talav is a lake in the Gopipura locality in Surat. It was built in circa 1510 CE by Malik Gopi, who was an affluent merchant and governor of Surat during the Mughal Empire. In 2012, the lake was renovated by Surat Municipal Corporation and the area surrounding it was redeveloped as a recreational facility.

Surat Castle

Between 1540 and 1546, Sultan Mahmud III had this castle built with extra strength for protection against Portugese raids. It is now filled with government offices, but you can go up to the top for a marvelous view of the city and Tapi river.
